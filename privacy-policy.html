üîë Login Methods & What We Access

Sign in with Apple

Data We Receive Your Control
Apple User ID (unique identifier) Always provided
Email Address You can choose to hide your email (Apple provides a relay address)

Apple Sign-In is the most private option. You can hide your real email, and Apple doesn't track your app usage.

Sign in with Google (Gmail)

Data We Receive Purpose
Google User ID Unique identifier for your account
Email Address Account identification & communication
Display Name Show your name in the app
Profile Photo URL Display your profile picture (optional)

We do NOT access: Your Gmail emails, Google Drive, Calendar, Contacts, or any other Google services.

Sign in with Phone Number

Data We Receive Purpose
Phone Number Account identification & verification
SMS Verification Code One-time verification (not stored)

Phone authentication is handled securely by Firebase. We store your phone number to identify your account but never share it with third parties.

What We Cache & Why

üîí Permissions We Request

Together requests minimal permissions. Here's what each permission does:

Permission When Asked What We Use It For
Push Notifications First app launch Notify you when your partner shares moods, drawings, or updates
Internet Access Always required Sync data with your partner in real-time

Permissions We DON'T Request

  • Camera: Not accessed (we don't take photos)
  • Photo Library: Not accessed (we don't upload images)
  • Location: Not accessed (Journey places are added manually via map)
  • Microphone: Not accessed (no voice features)
  • Contacts: Not accessed (partner connection via invite code)

We believe in minimal permissions. Together only asks for what's absolutely necessary to function.
